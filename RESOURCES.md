# Founder fundamentals — Resources

Curated, high-trust sources. Lessons draw knowledge from here, not from guesses. Pruned ruthlessly — better five sharp sources than thirty mediocre ones.

## Knowledge

### Pricing & monetization
- [Video: "Startup Pricing 101" — Kevin Hale, Y Combinator](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jwXlo9gy_k4)
  **The canonical primary source for pricing.** Introduces the pricing thermometer (cost / price / value), the four common pricing mistakes, value-based pricing, and the "raise price until you lose ~20%" rule of thumb. Use for: every pricing lesson. *(Lesson 01's primary source.)*
- [YC Library: Startup Pricing 101 (companion writeup)](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/6h-startup-pricing-101)
  Text companion to the talk. Use for: re-reading the framework without the video.

### Pricing AI products (variable compute cost)
- [Article: "How to Price AI Products: The Complete Guide for PMs (2026)" — Aakash Gupta](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/how-to-price-ai-products)
  Practical framework for AI pricing models & metrics. Use for: the EncoreShot per-asset-cost pricing puzzle.
- [Article: "The Hidden COGS of AI: Why Your Pricing Model Might Be Doomed" — Monetizely](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/the-hidden-cogs-of-ai-why-your-pricing-model-might-be-doomed)
  Why fixed-price AI features can bleed margin when usage is variable. Use for: understanding the floor under EncoreShot's price.
- [Article: "Subscription App Economics: The Hidden Cost of AI Features" — RevenueCat](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/ai-feature-cost-subscription-app-margins/)
  Real margin math for consumer apps shipping AI. Use for: hobbyist-tier economics. *(RevenueCat is a high-trust source on app subscription economics.)*
- [Article: "AI pricing explained: what AI actually costs in 2026" — CloudZero](https://www.cloudzero.com/blog/ai-pricing/)
  How providers charge and how inference cost behaves over time (≈10× cheaper/year). Use for: forecasting EncoreShot's cost floor. **Perishable — re-verify figures before any decision rests on them.**

- [Reference: Gemini API video understanding (token rates)](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/video-understanding) + [pricepertoken — Gemini Flash](https://pricepertoken.com/pricing-page/model/google-gemini-2.5-flash)
  First-party token-per-second rates + current Flash $/token. Use for: making EncoreShot's cost floor concrete. **Perishable — re-verify.** *(Lesson 02.)*

### Packaging & tiering
- [Article: "The Good-Better-Best Approach to Pricing" — Rafi Mohammed, HBR (2018)](https://hbr.org/2018/09/the-good-better-best-approach-to-pricing)
  The canonical piece on three-tier packaging and price fences. Use for: designing EncoreShot's tiers. *(Lesson 03's primary source.)*
- [Guide: "The 2026 Guide to SaaS, AI, and Agentic Pricing Models" — Monetizely](https://www.getmonetizely.com/blogs/the-2026-guide-to-saas-ai-and-agentic-pricing-models)
  Current landscape of subscription/usage/outcome/hybrid models. Use for: choosing a model blend.

### Customer discovery
- [Book: _The Mom Test_ — Rob Fitzpatrick](https://www.momtestbook.com/)
  The canonical guide to customer interviews that don't lie to you. Short, ~130pp. Use for: any "should I talk to users / how" question. *(Lesson 04's primary source.)*
- [Video: "How to Talk to Users" — Eric Migicovsky, YC](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MT4Ig2uqjTc)
  YC's 25-min distillation of the Mom Test. Use for: a fast refresher before interviews.

### Distribution & growth
- [Essay: "The Bullseye Framework for Getting Traction" — Gabriel Weinberg](https://medium.com/@yegg/the-bullseye-framework-for-getting-traction-ef49d05bfd7e)
  The free core of the book _Traction_ (19 channels, brainstorm→test→focus). Use for: choosing where to spend acquisition energy. *(Lesson 05's primary source.)*
- [Essay: "Do Things That Don't Scale" — Paul Graham](https://www.paulgraham.com/ds.html)
  Why the un-scalable is the early-stage strategy. Use for: justifying the concert-seeding motion.
- [Guide: Referral program best practices (2026) — Refgrow](https://refgrow.com/blog/referral-program-best-practices)
  Two-sided rewards, aha-moment timing, credit-vs-cash data. Use for: designing the voucher→referral loop.

### Startup fundamentals (idea → PMF → growth)
- [Y Combinator Startup Library](https://www.ycombinator.com/library)
  The single best free corpus of founder knowledge. Use for: almost any fundamentals question; we'll surface specific talks per lesson.
- [Paul Graham essays](https://www.paulgraham.com/articles.html) — esp. *"Do Things That Don't Scale"* and *"How to Get Startup Ideas."*
  Foundational founder mental models. Use for: mindset, early-stage tactics, what makes a startup.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/)
  Free, 50k+ members, build-in-public culture, real revenue numbers. **Best fit for EncoreShot's stage.** Use for: launch feedback, pricing gut-checks, accountability.
- [MicroConf Connect](https://microconfconnect.com/)
  Bootstrapped-SaaS founders since 2012; hot-seat calls give focused feedback on one problem. Use for: deeper, structured feedback once there's traction.
- [Hacker News — "Show HN"](https://news.ycombinator.com/show)
  YC's forum; the classic technical launch format. Use for: a launch moment + technical-audience feedback.
