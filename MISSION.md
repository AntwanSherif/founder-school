# Mission: Founder fundamentals — turning EncoreShot into a business

## Why
Antwan is a strong engineer with a near-zero background in business, building **EncoreShot** (an AI tool that culls concert photos/videos and exports social-ready files) solo. The goal is to monetize it — and possibly grow it into a real startup — *while learning the fundamentals*, so each decision (pricing, launch timing, acquisition) is made with confidence instead of guesswork. The win is not "understand business" in the abstract; it's **shipping a product that makes money, priced and marketed on purpose, not by accident.**

## Success looks like
- Can set a price for EncoreShot and defend it with a value-based rationale (not cost-plus).
- Understands the unit economics of an AI product where compute cost varies by asset (video duration, model choice) — and can keep the product profitable.
- Can run a zero-budget go-to-market motion (the concert-seeding + voucher flywheel already scoped) and read the signals it produces.
- Knows when EncoreShot is "MVP-ready," "ready to charge," and "ready to expand" — and can ask the teacher to assess the actual product against those bars (**advisor mode**).
- Has a working grasp of: pricing, customer discovery & distribution, AI-product economics, time-to-market, negotiation, referrals/affiliates, and what separates a project from a startup.

## Constraints
- **Solo founder, engineer-first, near-zero business background.** Teach from first principles; assume no prior vocabulary.
- **Deeper, less-frequent sessions** — fewer, meatier lessons over short daily drills.
- **Grounded in EncoreShot.** Lessons should lean on the real product context (see [reference/encoreshot-context.md](reference/encoreshot-context.md)) so future sessions don't re-grill for context that already exists. Some lessons may stay theory; many should apply directly to EncoreShot.
- **Returnable.** Antwan may come back in weeks/months and ask specific applied questions ("should I do referrals / vouchers?") expecting the project context to already be loaded.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Fundraising mechanics (term sheets, cap tables, VC process) — relevant only *if* the startup path opens; not the near-term focus.
- Hiring / team-building — this is a solo product.
- Deep accounting/tax/legal entity setup — touch lightly under "ops basics," don't go deep yet.
