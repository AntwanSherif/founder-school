# Teaching notes — preferences & working notes

## Learner profile
- **Engineer, near-zero business background.** Teach from first principles; define every term the first time. No assumed business vocabulary.
- Strong systems thinker — analogies to code/engineering land well and compress fast.

## Format preferences
- **Deeper, less-frequent lessons.** Meatier sessions over short daily drills. One solid tangible win per lesson is still the rule, but lessons can carry more than a single micro-concept.
- Lessons are HTML, beautiful/Tufte-like, kept in `lessons/`. He returns to them, so they must read well later.
- Keep a running **glossary** (`reference/glossary.html`) — every new business term goes there, and lessons link to it.

## Special modes requested
- **Advisor / consultant mode.** On request, look at EncoreShot's *actual* current shape (repo + `reference/encoreshot-context.md`) and assess: MVP-ready? ready to charge? ready to expand? This is a recurring lesson type, not a one-off.
- **Applied pricing for AI assets.** The variable-cost-per-asset problem (video duration, model choice) is a first-class recurring thread, not a footnote.
- **Returnable context.** He may come back weeks later with applied questions (referrals, vouchers). Project context is pre-loaded in `reference/encoreshot-context.md` — read it instead of re-grilling.
