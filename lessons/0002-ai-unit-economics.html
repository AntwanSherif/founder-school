The real floor under EncoreShot

Last lesson, cost set your price floor. This lesson we make that floor a real number — and watch it move as a clip gets longer, a model gets fancier, or a resolution gets higher.

In Lesson 01 I waved at "~$0.15 a month" as your hobbyist cost. Hand-wavy numbers are dangerous in pricing, so let's earn it. By the end you'll be able to estimate EncoreShot's COGS per user, know which knobs move it, and read whether a price keeps you in the black.

The one equation that matters: gross margin

Strip everything else away and a business lives or dies on one ratio: gross margin = (price − cost-to-deliver) ÷ price. Traditional software runs 70–90% because the cost of one more user is nearly zero. AI products run lower — often 20–60% — because every single use spends real compute. At scaling AI companies, inference alone averages ~23% of revenue (CloudZero, 2026). That compute line is your unit economics, and for you it's not fixed — it's variable per asset.

What actually drives EncoreShot's per-asset cost

Vision LLMs bill by tokens — chunks of input and output. A photo becomes ~1,000 input tokens; a video becomes tokens per second of footage. Current Gemini Flash rates make the levers visible (Google docs, perishable):

  • Clip duration — video costs ≈ 100 tokens/sec at low media resolution, ~300/sec at default. A 30-second clip costs 2× a 15-second one. Linear, and it adds up fast.
  • Media resolution & fps — "default" res is ~3× "low." Your scoring config picks this per call, so it's a direct cost dial.
  • Model choice — Gemini 2.5 Flash is ~$0.30 / million input tokens; 3.5 Flash is ~$1.50 (pricepertoken, 2026). Same job, 5× the bill. This is why you run A/B providers.
  • Output length — the Reasoning text + 5-dimension JSON. Output tokens cost ~8× input, so verbose prompts quietly hurt.

At ~2 shows/month that's ≈ $0.13/user/mo in AI — the "$0.15" was honest. But flip to default resolution and 3.5 Flash and the input lines roughly 15× → the floor jumps toward $0.50–1/mo. Same product, your config choices moved the floor 4–7×.

Two levers you already built

Tiered video scoring is a margin feature in disguise: the FFmpeg pre-filter throws out too-short / too-dark / silent clips before spending an LLM call. Every rejected clip is pure saved COGS. And A/B providers let you keep choosing the cheapest model that still scores well — directly defending the gap between price and cost.

The tailwind, and the trap

The wind is at your back: inference for equivalent quality has fallen roughly 10× per year (a16z / CloudZero). Today's floor is next year's rounding error. The trap (Jevons' paradox): as cost drops you'll do more AI per user — re-scoring, second models, richer video — so total spend can rise even as per-call cost falls. Watch cost per active user, not per call.

"For every $1M in AI product revenue, ~$230K is consumed by inference before you've paid for anything else." — CloudZero, 2026
